Achievements

Some of his major achievements throughout his career.

Cricketer of the Decade

Virat Kohli, a dominant figure in cricket from 2011 to 2020, showcased exceptional batting skills, breaking numerous records and earning the title of cricketer of the decade. His consistent performance and aggressive style made him a formidable force in international cricket, leaving an indelible mark on the sport.

Most ODI centuries ever

Virat Kohli's historic feat of surpassing Sachin Tendulkar with 50 ODI centuries solidifies his status as a cricketing icon, inspiring generations with his unparalleled mastery at the crease. This milestone encapsulates his relentless pursuit of excellence and cements his legacy as one of cricket's greatest batsmen.

Arjuna Award for Cricket

Kohli received the Arjuna Award, India's second highest sporting honor, from the government of India in 2013. In 2017 Kohli garnered the Padma Shri, India's fourth highest civilian award. In 2018 he received the Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna Award (now the Major Dhyan Chand Khel Ratna Award), India's highest sporting honor.

Career Highlights

A Journey of Triumphs and Challenges: Virat Kohli's Career Milestones.

Early Years and Domestic Cricket (1988-2006):
Born on November 5, 1988, in Delhi to Prem Kohli. Joined West Delhi Cricket Academy in 1998. Made his Ranji Trophy debut for Delhi in 2006. Father passed away on December 19, 2006, during his match against Karnataka.
Emergence in Domestic and U-19 Cricket (2007-2009):
Played first T20 match in 2007. Captained the U-19 Indian Cricket team to victory in the 2008 World Cup. Made his ODI debut in 2008. Scored his first ODI century in December 2009.
Establishment in International Cricket (2010-2012):
Became the fastest Indian to score 1,000 runs in ODIs. Excelled in the 2011 ICC World Cup, scoring a century in his debut match. Made Test debut in 2011. Named vice-captain for the 2012 Asia Cup.
Consolidation and Leadership (2013 onwards):
Took over as captain of Royal Challengers Bangalore in IPL 2013. Named ODI Cricketer of the Year by ICC in 2012. Continued to excel both as a batsman and leader in international and domestic cricket. Developed into one of the world's premier cricketers, earning numerous accolades and records.
